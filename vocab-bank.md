# 單字庫（累積所有學過的單字）

## 2026-07-03

### monarchy /ˈmɒnərki/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a form of government with a hereditary ruler, typically a king or queen
- **中文:** 君主制
- *"Constitutional monarchy replaced absolute rule in much of 19th-century Europe."*
  立憲君主制在十九世紀的歐洲多數地方取代了君主專制。

### aristocracy /ˌærɪˈstɒkrəsi/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the highest class in certain societies, especially hereditary nobility
- **中文:** 貴族階層
- *"The French Revolution stripped the aristocracy of many of its ancient privileges."*
  法國大革命剝奪了貴族階層許多古老的特權。

### absolutism /ˈæbsəluːtɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a political system in which a ruler holds unrestricted power
- **中文:** 專制主義
- *"Louis XIV epitomized the age of absolutism in European history."*
  路易十四是歐洲歷史上專制主義時代的典型代表。

### colonialism /kəˈloʊniəlɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the policy of acquiring control over other territories and peoples
- **中文:** 殖民主義
- *"The legacy of colonialism still shapes political boundaries across Africa and Asia."*
  殖民主義的遺緒至今仍形塑著非洲與亞洲的政治疆界。

### secularism /ˈsɛkjələrɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the principle of separating religion from civic and political affairs
- **中文:** 世俗主義
- *"The Enlightenment gradually pushed European governance toward secularism."*
  啟蒙運動逐漸將歐洲的治理推向世俗主義。

### humanism /ˈhjuːmənɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** an intellectual movement emphasizing human potential and achievement, especially in the Renaissance
- **中文:** 人文主義
- *"Renaissance humanism placed human potential and achievement at the center of intellectual life."*
  文藝復興人文主義將人類的潛能與成就置於知識生活的核心。

### schism /ˈsɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a split or division between strongly opposed groups, often religious
- **中文:** 分裂
- *"The Great Schism of 1054 divided Christianity into Eastern and Western churches."*
  1054 年的教會大分裂使基督教分裂為東西兩派教會。

### reformation /ˌrɛfərˈmeɪʃən/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a movement for religious or political reform, especially 16th-century Christianity
- **中文:** 宗教改革
- *"The Reformation permanently fractured the religious unity of Western Europe."*
  宗教改革永久地打破了西歐的宗教統一。

### despot /ˈdɛspət/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a ruler with absolute power, often exercised cruelly or oppressively
- **中文:** 專制君主；暴君
- *"Historians still debate whether Peter the Great should be remembered as a reformer or a despot."*
  史學家至今仍在爭論彼得大帝該被視為改革者還是暴君。

### utopia /juːˈtoʊpiə/ — n.
- **英文定義:** an imagined place or state in which everything is perfect
- **中文:** 烏托邦
- *"Thomas More's Utopia imagined an ideal society governed by reason."*
  湯瑪斯·摩爾的《烏托邦》構想了一個由理性治理的理想社會。

### dystopia /dɪsˈtoʊpiə/ — n.
- **英文定義:** an imagined society characterized by suffering, injustice, or oppression
- **中文:** 反烏托邦
- *"Many 20th-century novels envisioned dystopia as a warning against totalitarianism."*
  許多二十世紀的小說將反烏托邦作為對極權主義的警示。

### anachronism /əˈnækrənɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** something that seems out of place in the historical period it belongs to
- **中文:** 時代錯置
- *"Depicting medieval knights with firearms would be a glaring anachronism."*
  把中世紀騎士描繪成拿著槍械會是明顯的時代錯置。

### eloquent /ˈɛləkwənt/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** fluent and persuasive in speaking or writing
- **中文:** 雄辯的；辭藻優美的
- *"Churchill's eloquent wartime speeches rallied a nation under siege."*
  邱吉爾在戰時雄辯滔滔的演說振奮了一個被圍困的國家。

### erudite /ˈɛrjʊdaɪt/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** having or showing great knowledge or learning
- **中文:** 博學的
- *"The professor's erudite lectures on classical antiquity drew students from every department."*
  這位教授關於古典時代的博學講座吸引了各系學生前來聽講。

### venerable /ˈvɛnərəbəl/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** accorded great respect, especially because of age, wisdom, or character
- **中文:** 令人尊敬的；德高望重的
- *"Oxford remains one of Europe's most venerable centers of learning."*
  牛津仍是歐洲最受尊崇的學術重鎮之一。

### quintessential /ˌkwɪntɪˈsɛnʃəl/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** representing the most perfect or typical example of a quality or class
- **中文:** 典型的；精髓的
- *"Vienna is often considered the quintessential city of the Habsburg imagination."*
  維也納常被視為哈布斯堡想像中最典型的城市。

### Byzantine /ˈbɪzəntiːn/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** relating to the Byzantine Empire; or excessively complex and intricate
- **中文:** 拜占庭的；錯綜複雜的
- *"The empire's court politics grew so Byzantine that few outsiders could follow them."*
  這個帝國的宮廷政治變得如此錯綜複雜，幾乎沒有外人能夠理解。

### Machiavellian /ˌmækiəˈvɛliən/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics
- **中文:** 權謀的；馬基維利式的
- *"Court intrigue in Renaissance Italy was often described as thoroughly Machiavellian."*
  文藝復興時期義大利的宮廷陰謀常被形容為十足的權謀算計。

### requiem /ˈrɛkwiəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a mass or musical composition for the dead; an act of solemn remembrance
- **中文:** 安魂曲
- *"Mozart's unfinished Requiem has become one of the most haunting works in Western music."*
  莫札特未完成的《安魂曲》已成為西方音樂中最扣人心弦的作品之一。

### pantheon /ˈpænθiən/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a group of illustrious or notable people, or the deities of a religion collectively
- **中文:** 眾神殿；名人殿堂
- *"Voltaire is enshrined in the Pantheon alongside other giants of French thought."*
  伏爾泰與其他法國思想巨擘一同安葬於先賢祠。

## 2026-07-02

### hegemony /hɪˈdʒɛməni/ — n.
- **英文定義:** dominance of one state, group, or idea over others
- **中文:** 霸權
- *"For centuries, Rome's hegemony extended across the Mediterranean world."*
  羅馬的霸權在數個世紀裡擴及整個地中海世界。

### feudalism /ˈfjuːdəlɪzəm/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a social system based on lords, vassals, and land tenure, dominant in medieval Europe
- **中文:** 封建制度
- *"Feudalism shaped the social and political structure of medieval Europe."*
  封建制度形塑了中世紀歐洲的社會與政治結構。

### annexation /ˌænɛkˈseɪʃən/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the action of forcibly adding a territory to one's own
- **中文:** 兼併；併吞
- *"The annexation of Alsace-Lorraine remained a source of tension between France and Germany."*
  亞爾薩斯-洛林的兼併問題，長期是法德之間緊張關係的根源。

### sovereignty /ˈsɒvrɪnti/ — n.
- **英文定義:** supreme power or authority held by a state
- **中文:** 主權
- *"The Peace of Westphalia is often credited with establishing the modern concept of state sovereignty."*
  西發里亞和約常被認為確立了現代國家主權的概念。

### partition /pɑːrˈtɪʃən/ — n./v.
- **英文定義:** the division of a country into separate, self-governing parts
- **中文:** （國家的）分割；瓜分
- *"The partition of Poland in the late 18th century erased the country from the map for over a century."*
  十八世紀末波蘭遭到瓜分，使這個國家在地圖上消失了一個多世紀。

### zeitgeist /ˈtsaɪtɡaɪst/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the defining spirit or mood of a particular period in history
- **中文:** 時代精神
- *"Goethe's works are often seen as capturing the zeitgeist of Romantic-era Germany."*
  歌德的作品常被視為捕捉了浪漫主義時期德國的時代精神。

### Enlightenment /ɪnˈlaɪtənmənt/ — n. (historical period)
- **英文定義:** the 18th-century intellectual movement emphasizing reason and individualism over tradition
- **中文:** 啟蒙運動
- *"Enlightenment thinkers like Voltaire championed reason over superstition."*
  像伏爾泰這樣的啟蒙運動思想家推崇理性、反對迷信。

### Renaissance /ˈrɛnəsɑːns/ — n. (historical period)
- **英文定義:** the revival of art, literature, and learning in Europe from the 14th to 16th century
- **中文:** 文藝復興
- *"The Italian Renaissance produced masters such as Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci."*
  義大利文藝復興孕育了米開朗基羅和達文西等大師。

### decadence /ˈdɛkədəns/ — n.
- **英文定義:** moral or cultural decline, often associated with self-indulgence
- **中文:** 頹廢；衰落
- *"Historians often describe the late Roman Empire in terms of decadence and decline."*
  史學家常用頹廢與衰落來描述羅馬帝國晚期。

### baroque /bəˈroʊk/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** highly ornate and extravagant in style, especially in art and architecture
- **中文:** 巴洛克風格的
- *"Versailles is a quintessential example of baroque architecture."*
  凡爾賽宮是巴洛克建築的典型代表。

### melancholic /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪk/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** having or expressing a pensive, thoughtful sadness
- **中文:** 憂鬱的；感傷的
- *"The ruins evoked a melancholic sense of empires long vanished."*
  這些遺跡喚起一種對早已消逝帝國的憂鬱感懷。

### nostalgic /nɒˈstældʒɪk/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** feeling or expressing wistful affection for the past
- **中文:** 懷舊的
- *"There is a nostalgic longing in much of European literature for a lost aristocratic age."*
  許多歐洲文學中都帶有對逝去貴族時代的懷舊渴望。

### sublime /səˈblaɪm/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** of such excellence, beauty, or grandeur as to inspire awe
- **中文:** 崇高的；壯麗的
- *"Romantic painters sought to capture the sublime power of nature."*
  浪漫主義畫家試圖捕捉大自然那種令人敬畏的崇高力量。

### ephemeral /ɪˈfɛmərəl/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** lasting for a very short time
- **中文:** 短暫的；朝生暮死的
- *"Empires, however powerful, often prove ephemeral against the span of history."*
  帝國無論多麼強盛，放在歷史長河中往往顯得短暫。

### evocative /ɪˈvɒkətɪv/ — adj.
- **英文定義:** bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind
- **中文:** 引發聯想的；喚起回憶的
- *"The old quarter of Prague is remarkably evocative of its Habsburg past."*
  布拉格舊城區極為喚起人們對哈布斯堡王朝過往的聯想。

### epoch /ˈɛpək/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a period of time in history marked by notable events or characteristics
- **中文:** 時代；紀元
- *"The fall of Constantinople in 1453 marked the end of one epoch and the beginning of another."*
  1453 年君士坦丁堡的陷落標誌著一個時代的終結與另一個時代的開始。

### oeuvre /ˈɜːvrə/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the complete body of work produced by an artist, writer, or composer
- **中文:** （藝術家的）全部作品
- *"Rembrandt's oeuvre spans portraits, biblical scenes, and self-studies."*
  林布蘭的全部作品涵蓋肖像畫、聖經場景與自畫像研究。

### allegory /ˈæləɡɔːri/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a story, poem, or image with a hidden symbolic or moral meaning
- **中文:** 寓言；寓意
- *"Dante's Divine Comedy functions as an allegory of the soul's journey toward God."*
  但丁的《神曲》可視為靈魂朝向上帝旅程的一則寓言。

### antiquity /ænˈtɪkwɪti/ — n.
- **英文定義:** the ancient past, especially the periods of Greek and Roman civilization
- **中文:** 古代；古典時代
- *"Scholars of antiquity still debate the causes of Rome's fall."*
  研究古代史的學者至今仍在爭論羅馬衰亡的原因。

### iconoclast /aɪˈkɒnəklæst/ — n.
- **英文定義:** a person who attacks or challenges cherished beliefs or institutions
- **中文:** 打破傳統者；反傳統者
- *"Martin Luther is remembered as a religious iconoclast who challenged the authority of the Church."*
  馬丁路德被視為挑戰教會權威的宗教反傳統者。
